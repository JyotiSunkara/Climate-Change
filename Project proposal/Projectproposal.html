OVERVIEW AND PURPOSE

Climate Change is the defining issue of our time and we are at a defining moment. There are some basic well-established scientific links like

  • The concentration of greenhouse gases in the earth’s atmosphere is directly linked to the global temperatures on Earth
  • The concentration has been rising steadily, and global temperatures along with it, since the time of the Industrial Revolution (1760 – 1840)

However, till this day conspiracy theorists typically allege that, through worldwide acts of professional and criminal misconduct, the science behind global warming has been invented or distorted for ideological or financial reasons.

The aim of this data visualization is to find climate change buried in millions of data points obtained from a verified source.

DATA SOURCE

The Global Historical Climatology Network (GHCN) is an integrated database of climate summaries from land surface stations across the globe. GHCN-Daily contains records from over 100,000 stations in 180 countries and territories.

Main website: https://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/data-access/land-based-station-data/land-based-datasets/global-historical-climatology-network-ghcn

FTP Database for daily data: ftp://ftp.ncdc.noaa.gov/pub/data/ghcn/daily/

Readme: ftp://ftp.ncdc.noaa.gov/pub/data/ghcn/v3/README

DATA VALIDITY

 Data from the stations across the globe have been subjected to a common suite of quality assurance reviews. The data are obtained from more than 20 sources. Some data are more than 175 years old while others are less than an hour old.

Every day there are updates to GHCN-Daily station data from a variety of data streams, which also undergo a suite of quality checks. In addition the dataset is reconstructed each weekend from its 25-plus data source components to ensure that GHCN-Daily is generally in sync with its growing list of constituent sources. During this process, the system applies quality assurance checks to the full dataset.

DATA REPRESENTATIONS

Next, we want to represent the data trend that will solidify the reality of climate change. Here, we want to show a map of the world with a slider moving from the 1980s to present. On the map for every day, a blue dot will appear if the previously set minimum temperature was broken on that day at that particular weather station. A red dot will appear if the previously set maximum was broken on that day at that particular weather station on the map.

Each dot will show the highest or lowest maximum or minimum temperature ever recorded at a station till that day. Around 40,000 all-time records were set in the last two decades, out of 500 million daily minimum and maximum entries!

This visualization will show that the records for maximum and minimum temperatures have been broken repeatedly year after year hence proving that climate change is a reality that we need to find a solution to.
